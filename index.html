Project 2:
Performance Investigation of Developed Rice Variant Classifier


Description:

The Rice Variant Classifier project aims to assist the rice industry in Tacurong City, Sultan Kudarat, by using image processing to classify different rice variants.


×
Rice Variant Classifier Project:

The system combines hardware and software, where rice samples are manually placed in the machine, and computer vision is used to classify the variants. Initially, a webcam was used, but due to limited resolution, a cellphone camera was adopted for improved accuracy. The classifier achieved a 99% success rate, proving to be an efficient solution for identifying rice variants.


General Objective:
Create a rice classifier model to categorize rice seedlings into distinct variants based on visual attributes.


Specific Objectives:

  • Use a Machine Learning Algorithm to develop the classification model.
  • Classify rice seedlings into RC160, RC438, and RC440 varieties.
  • Display the quality percentage of the classification.
  • Evaluate the system for accuracy, precision, recall, and acceptability.

Significance of the Study:
The project aids the rice industry in Tacurong City by enabling farmers, buyers, and sellers to classify rice variants efficiently. It simplifies identifying rice types like RC160, RC438, and RC440 using image processing.


Scope and Limitation:
The project focuses on image processing and deep learning for classifying the three rice variants, with testing conducted in Tacurong City during the second semester of 2023-2024. The limitation is that only RC160, RC438, and RC440 will be classified, while other varieties will be marked as unidentified.
